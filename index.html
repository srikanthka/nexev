Why the BMS?

Four engineering pillars that separate this from any off-the-shelf module.

Automotive-Grade Safety

Dual-layer protection — TI BQ76952 hardware faults trip in under 400µs before firmware even runs. A software SafetyEngine polls every 100ms as a second independent watchdog. Both must agree for the pack to operate.

OVP / UVP SCD < 400µs OCD OTP / UTP Pre-charge
Black Box Logging

Every fault, every spike, every anomaly — timestamped and written to flash. No more guessing why a pack failed in the field. Pull the log, replay the event, trace the root cause. Field diagnostics without a lab.

Fault Logs Flash Storage Timestamped Field Diagnostics
Smart Connectivity

CAN bus with 29-bit extended IDs — 8 live telemetry frames covering pack voltage, per-cell voltages, temperatures, SoC, current, and fault status. Built for integration with motor controllers, vehicle ECUs, and IoT gateways.

CAN 2.0B 8 PGNs UART Debug SPI / I²C
One Board, Endless Applications

From micro-mobility to industrial robotics — the Gen-1 is designed as a platform, not a product. 10S–16S configuration, 140mm × 60mm compact footprint, firmware-selectable cell chemistry. One BMS, many vehicles.

10S–16S NMC / LFP 140 × 60 mm OEM Ready

Regulatory Services

India Market Entry.
Done Right.

NexEV operates as an Authorised Indian Representative (AIR) under the BIS Act 2016 — managing end-to-end certification, customs logistics, and post-market compliance for foreign manufacturers entering the Indian market.

Why Foreign Manufacturers Need an AIR

The Bureau of Indian Standards mandates that every foreign manufacturer seeking BIS Certification must appoint a resident Indian company as their Authorised Indian Representative. The AIR bears full legal responsibility for certification compliance, product recalls, and market surveillance obligations — making selection of the right AIR a critical strategic decision, not a formality.
